**Vibe:** Professional Editorial / High-End GRC Software.

## 3. Features & Logic Explanation

### A. Asset Catalog (The Source-of-Truth)
*   **What it is:** A database of all organizational tools, data, and systems.
*   **How it works:** Users "enumerate" assets, giving them an importance score (1-5).
*   **Why it matters:** NIST says you can't manage risk if you don't know what you're protecting.

### B. Risk Calculation Matrix (The Engine)
*   **Logic:** `Likelihood (Chance) × Impact (Damage) = Risk Score (1-25)`.
*   **Methodology:** Maps real-world threat vectors (Phishing, SQL Injection) to system vulnerabilities.
*   **NIST Framework:** Implements the formal assessment phase of NIST 800-30.

### C. 5x5 Heatmap Visualization
*   **Visual:** A density-aware color grid showing where risks are concentrated.
*   **Utility:** High-level executives use this to instantly see if the "top-right" (Critical) quadrant is empty.

### D. Automated Reporting (The Professional Output)
*   **Deliverable:** A formal Security Memorandum.
*   **Context:** Automatically maps risks to NIST 800-53 and ISO 27001 controls.
*   **Innovation:** Converts technical data into business-ready documentation.

### E. Security Guard (SIM Auth)
*   **Feature:** A formal login interface.
*   **Purpose:** Protects the sensitive GRC data from unauthorized "view-only" access.

## 4. NIST Framework & Integration
**NIST SP 800-30** is the gold standard for conducting information security risk assessments.
GuardianRisk uses it by:
1.  **Phase 1:** Characterizing the system (Asset Catalog).
2.  **Phase 2:** Identifying threats and vulnerabilities (Threat Library).
3.  **Phase 3:** Determining Likelihood and Impact (Calculated Matrix).
4.  **Phase 4:** Determining Risk Level (Risk Register).

## 5. Professional Example: "GlobalRetail Corp"
Imagine a company like Amazon. 
1.  **Asset:** "Customer Credit Card Database" (Importance: 5/5).
2.  **Threat:** "SQL Injection Attack".
3.  **Calculation:** Chance is 3 (Medium), but Damage is 5 (Extreme).
4.  **Result:** Risk Score of 15 (HIGH).
5.  **Output:** The report tells GlobalRetail to "implement database encryption and input validation" (NIST Rule: AC-3).

## 6. What happens if a feature is removed?
- **No Asset Catalog:** You lose the ability to judge *priority*. Tracking risks on a generic "Server" is less effective than tracking it on the "Payroll Server."
- **No Heatmap:** Large companies would be overwhelmed by lists. Without a heatmap, you can't "see" the patterns of danger.
- **No Reports:** The IT team might know the risks, but the Board of Directors won't have a document to sign, meaning no budget for security fixes.
